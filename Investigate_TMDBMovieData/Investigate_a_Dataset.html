Introduction¶

Tmdb is a database which contains various details about all kinds of movies and TVs, such as genres, rating, cast, crew and etc. The dataset used in this project is taken from Kaggle, and I'll analyze it to gain some insight about the film industry with the following questions -

  • What genres are more popular than others? Do they gain more popularity? Do they have more votes? Do they get better ratings? Do they generate more revenue?
  • What factors drive a movie's success (in revenune): some speicifc genres? greater budget? released in specific months?

    Conclusion of the analysis of TMDb movie data¶

    Conclusion¶

    • We can't predict the rating, the popularity and the revenue of a movie based on the popularity of its genre
    • To generate greater revenue (not profit), we may
      • Invest in the following genres: Adventure, Fantasy, Animation, Action, Family
      • Target the release date to summer and holiday season
      • Increase budget

    Limitation¶

    Missing and erroneous value in dataset¶

    This dataset has lots of missing and erroneous data, which may distort our perception on distributions of some key variables, and hence may lead to biased conclusion.

  • Total of 5696 observations have ZERO budget Total of 139 observations have less than 1 million budget, which is implausible (though possible)
  • Total of 6016 observations have ZERO revenue
  • Total of 23 observations have NULL genres

    • In this analysis, the observations which have missing and/or erornous values are simply dropped. For more accurate analysis, we can visit Imdb and/or movies' home pages to double check on numbers, which is not an easy job.

    Limited analysis on "what factors drive a movie's success (in revenue)¶

    As a consumer, I have preference over some 'cast'/'director' and 'production_companies'. More objectively and more likely, more established 'production_companies' have more budget to hire well-known 'cast' and 'director' to make movies in more popular genres which generate more revenues. However, these variables are not analyzed.
